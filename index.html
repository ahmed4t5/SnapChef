Tonkotsu Ramen

Fukuoka, Japan

Invented Approx. 1940s
Calories ~850 kcal
Fun Culinary Fact

The deep milky, incredibly rich broth is achieved specifically by boiling select pork marrow bones for up to 18 hours.

Introduction to the Recipe

Tonkotsu ramen (豚骨ラーメン) is an ultra-savory noodle soup dish with a heavy, thick broth created from marrow bones, fat, and collagen. Enjoyed globally, it requires persistent temperature control to perfectly emulsify the pork fat into the broth base.
